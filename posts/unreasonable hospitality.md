Just finished reading Unreasonable Hospitality by Will Guidara. I think you should read it too.

A few topics really resonated with me:
- **the rule of 95/5**: budget 95% of your business obsessively, so you can spend the 5% lavishly
- **people never forget feelings**: you can forget experiences but never what they made you feel

which if you think about are truly related to each other. 

The 5% allows you to make people feel special. Will did this by using cheaper similar tasting wine over the course of a meal but then splurging on the last wine. You end feeling great because you just had a great wine. [Peak-end rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peak%E2%80%93end_rule). It's likely you might forget what you ate but not how you felt (tying into the 2nd topic).

Gonna think about how this can be implemented in software.

- "Language is how you give intention to your intuition and how you share your vision with others."
- "**Criticize the behavior, not the person. Praise in public; criticize in private. Praise with emotion, criticize without emotion.**"
- "Fads fade and cycle, but the human desire to be taken care of never goes away."
